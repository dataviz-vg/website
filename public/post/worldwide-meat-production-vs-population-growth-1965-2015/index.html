üìà Visualising the data

Method

In this visualization, we have taken both the datasets (Livestock Primary and Annual Population) and joined them by year, then only kept the date each 5 years. Then, the Meat column (expressed in kilograms) is divided by the Population column (expressed in number of humans). That gives us an estimation of how many kilograms of meats are produced on these given years for each human.

For example, in 1965, the volume of meat produced worldwide was 84,465,228,000, and the worldwide population 3,339,583,597, making an estimate of 25.29214 kilogram of meat by human on earth.

In 2015, the volume of meat produced worldwide was 325,427,235,000, and the worldwide population 7,379,797,139, making an estimate of 44.09704 kilogram of meat by human on earth.
